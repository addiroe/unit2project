Teenage Mental Health Crisis

What can you do to help?

About

According to the CDC's "Youth Risk Behavior Surveillance Data Summary & Trends Report," in 2021, more than 4 out of 10 teenage students said they felt persistently sad or hopeless, and nearly 1/3 say they experienced poor mental health. To add to this alarming data, almost 1 out of every 5 students seriously considered suicide, with 1 out of 10 attempting.

With these numbers growing rapidly, it is VITAL that those involved in children and teenagers lives know how to both prevent and assist with mental health issues before it's too late.

How Can You Help?

Your role in helping teenagers entirely depends on your relation to them. See below for how to help based on your relation:

Another Student:

For students, it is vital to speak up if help is needed. While this can be difficult at times, it is vital that you have a safe space to be able to speak to if necessary. It is very important to attempt to stay positive and stay connected with important people in your life that can help you.

Parent:

For parents, one of the most crucial things is to create a safe space for your teenager to be able to talk openly about what they're experiencing. Attempt to model healthy coping skills, and provide positive encouragement when needed.

Teacher:

For teachers, one of the most important things to take notice of are the signs of a mental health issue. One of the biggest changes to look for is a drastic change in personality. Be sure to encourage a positive environment and develop effective mental health resources within your school.

If other:

Depending on your relation to teenagers, this can mean many different things for how you approach your assistance. Most of the time, just creating a safe space for the teenagers to openly speak about their experiences is the most important thing overall.
